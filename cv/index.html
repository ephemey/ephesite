About Me




Due to my father's work in the oil industry, my family relocated to Aberdeen from China in 2005. Although we originally intended to stay for only a year or two, my mother and I quickly became accustomed to this new environment. The Western style of education was a pleasant contrast to the mechanical Chinese grind. As I began to pick up a wide range of hobbies and interests, my father returned to China alone for work. Soon after, I left Aberdeen to attend university in Glasgow.


Even as one of my parents is across the country and the other across the globe, they have both supported me as much as they could. After a year of familiarising myself with this city and settling in to university life, I hope to find a part-time position to increase my financial independence with the flexibility to allow my ongoing studies and interests.
